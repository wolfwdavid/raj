New Zealand's food-safety authority has updated the list of permitted hemp-derived food products, expanding approved uses under existing food standards, according to an official update.

## What changed

The update describes newly permitted product categories and the labeling requirements that apply. Officials quoted in the notice said the products fall under low-THC hemp standards distinct from other cannabis rules.

## Industry context

Hemp-food producers cited by business media said the change could broaden the range of products available to manufacturers. The authority noted that standard food-safety and labeling obligations continue to apply.

Guidance for producers on the updated categories was published alongside the notice.
