Germany has begun the second phase of its cannabis legislation, which turns from home cultivation and non-profit associations toward regulated commercial pilot projects in selected regions, according to the health ministry.

## What changes in phase two

Ministry documents describe time-limited retail trials that participating municipalities may apply to host. The trials are designed to be studied by academic institutions, the documents state.

> "The pilots are structured as scientific projects with reporting requirements," a ministry statement said.

## Regional interest

Several city governments have signaled interest, according to local reporting, though officials cautioned that applications must clear administrative review first. Researchers quoted by public broadcasters said the data-collection design is a central feature of the phase.

The ministry said further guidance on application criteria would be published in the coming period.
