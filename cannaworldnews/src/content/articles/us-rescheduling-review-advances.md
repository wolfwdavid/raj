A proposal to move cannabis to a less restrictive federal schedule has advanced to an administrative hearing, according to filings published in the Federal Register. The step opens a formal record in which registered participants submit written testimony and cross-examine expert witnesses.

## What the filing says

The published notice lists the parties granted standing, which include trade associations, patient-advocacy organizations, and several state regulators. Officials quoted in the notice described the hearing as a procedural stage rather than a final decision.

> "This is the part of the process where the evidence gets tested on the record," a spokesperson for one participating association said in a statement.

## Why observers are watching

Analysts cited by industry trade press say a reclassification would change how licensed businesses are taxed under existing federal code. Legal scholars interviewed by regional outlets cautioned that any change would take effect only after the full rulemaking concludes.

State-level programs would continue to operate under their own statutes regardless of the federal outcome, regulators noted in the record.
