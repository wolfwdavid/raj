South African authorities have published a draft framework outlining how a commercial cannabis sector might be regulated, building on legislation that addressed private adult use, according to a government notice.

## What the draft addresses

The document describes licensing tiers, small-grower provisions, and quality standards. Officials quoted in the notice said the framework aims to bring existing informal activity into a regulated system.

## Reaction from growers

Small-scale cultivators' representatives told regional outlets they want the rules to include pathways for existing farmers. Industry groups cited in business press welcomed clarity but sought detail on timelines.

The government invited written submissions and said it would hold consultations before finalizing the rules.
