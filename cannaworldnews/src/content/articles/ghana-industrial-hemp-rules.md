Ghana has published regulations establishing licensing conditions for the cultivation of industrial hemp below a specified THC threshold, according to a notice from the responsible authority.

## The conditions

The rules describe application requirements, permitted uses, and testing obligations to confirm crops stay under the defined limit. Officials quoted in the notice said the framework distinguishes industrial hemp from other cannabis categories.

## Economic framing

Agricultural analysts cited by business outlets said the rules could open opportunities in fiber and seed markets. Prospective growers told local media they were awaiting detail on where testing would be carried out.

The authority said guidance on the application process would follow the published regulations.
