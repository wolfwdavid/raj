Colombia has issued a decree broadening the categories of cannabis products that licensed producers may export, adding dried flower to a list previously focused on extracts, according to the official gazette.

## The policy change

The decree sets out documentation and quality-testing requirements that shipments must meet before leaving the country. Government officials cited in the announcement framed the change as part of a wider agricultural-export strategy.

## Industry response

Producer associations quoted by business press said the change could affect planting decisions for the coming season. Trade analysts noted that destination markets set their own import rules, which shipments must also satisfy.

The agriculture ministry said it would publish implementation guidance for license holders in the following weeks.
