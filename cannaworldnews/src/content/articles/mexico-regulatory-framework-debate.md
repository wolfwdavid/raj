A committee in Mexico's congress has resumed drafting a regulatory framework for adult cannabis use, following a series of court rulings directing lawmakers to act, according to legislative records.

## Background

Supreme Court decisions over several years found parts of the existing prohibition unconstitutional and instructed the legislature to write implementing rules. Progress has stalled multiple times, the records show.

## What the draft covers

Committee documents outline licensing categories, cultivation limits for personal use, and a proposed oversight body. Legislators quoted in committee minutes said unresolved questions include taxation and the role of existing agricultural agencies.

Civil-society groups tracking the process told regional outlets they would monitor whether the final text matches the court's directions. A committee vote has not been scheduled.
