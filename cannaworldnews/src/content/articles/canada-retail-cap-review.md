Ontario's cannabis regulator has opened a public consultation on the province's cap limiting how many storefronts a single operator may hold, according to a notice on the agency's website.

## The current rule

Under the existing framework, operators face a ceiling on total licenses, a measure introduced during the market's early rollout. The consultation document asks stakeholders whether that ceiling still serves its original purpose now that hundreds of stores are open.

## Stakeholder positions

Submissions summarized in the consultation portal show independent retailers and larger chains taking different views. Independent operators quoted by trade media said the cap preserves competition, while some larger operators argued it limits efficiency.

The regulator said it will publish a summary of responses before proposing any change. No timeline for a decision was given in the notice.
